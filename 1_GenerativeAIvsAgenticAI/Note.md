# Understanding Generative AI and Agentic AI: A New Era in HR Recruitment

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has rapidly evolved over the last few years, reshaping various industries. Among its many branches, **Generative AI** and **Agentic AI** stand out for their transformative impact, particularly in recruitment and HR processes. This blog post will explain the fundamental differences between these two AI paradigms and show how agentic AI can revolutionise hiring workflows by offering proactive, autonomous solutions.

---

## What is Generative AI?

Generative AI refers to a class of AI models capable of creating new content such as text, images, audio, code, and videos that closely resemble human-created data. Emerging prominently in the last three years, this technology has demonstrated remarkable ability to mimic human creativity and produce refined outputs.

### Key Characteristics of Generative AI

- **Content Creation Focus:** Generative AI’s primary goal is to generate new, human-like content based on learned data distributions.
- **Reactive Nature:** It responds to specific prompts or queries but does not initiate actions by itself.
- **Modalities:** It operates across various modalities — textual, visual, audio, and more.
  
### Popular Examples of Generative AI

- **ChatGPT:** A conversational agent capable of generating human-like text.
- **Image Generation Models:** Tools like DALL·E and Midjourney create images from textual descriptions.
- **Code Generation AI:** Models like Code Llama assist in writing and debugging software code.
- **Text-to-Speech (TTS):** Systems that convert text into natural-sounding speech, e.g., 11 Labs.
- **Video Generation:** AI that produces short videos from textual input, like Sora.

### Generative AI vs Traditional AI

Traditional AI models focus on identifying patterns between input and output in labelled data for tasks like classification and regression. In contrast, generative AI learns the overall distribution of data and generates entirely new samples that fit that distribution.

---

## Agentic AI: The Next Step Beyond Generative AI

While generative AI is powerful, it remains **reactive** and primarily content-focused. Agentic AI, on the other hand, is designed to be **proactive, autonomous, and goal-oriented**. It’s not just about generating content but about achieving a specified goal by planning, reasoning, and executing multiple steps.

### Defining Agentic AI

Agentic AI involves AI agents that:

- Receive a high-level goal.
- Plan a multi-step sequence to achieve the goal.
- Autonomously execute actions, including interactions with external tools and systems.
- Maintain context and memory over time to adapt to changes.

### Why Agentic AI?

Generative AI models require human intervention at every step, limiting efficiency in complex workflows. Agentic AI addresses this by:

- Initiating next steps without waiting for explicit instructions.
- Remembering prior interactions and context.
- Adapting strategies based on real-time feedback.
- Integrating with external APIs and tools to perform real-world tasks autonomously.

# Practical Application: AI in HR Recruitment

Imagine you are an HR recruiter tasked with hiring a backend engineer. Traditionally, this process involves drafting job descriptions (JDs), posting on job portals, shortlisting candidates, scheduling interviews, sending offer letters, and onboarding — a time-consuming and repetitive series of tasks.

Let's explore how both generative AI and agentic AI can assist in this process.

---

## Step-by-Step Recruitment Using Generative AI

1. **Drafting Job Description:**  
   You ask a generative AI chatbot to create a JD based on your inputs. The chatbot generates a generic JD for a backend engineer with specified experience.

2. **Job Posting:**  
   You manually post this JD on platforms like LinkedIn.

3. **Shortlisting Candidates:**  
   You receive applications and manually shortlist candidates by reviewing resumes.

4. **Interview Scheduling:**  
   You draft emails to candidates to invite them for interviews.

5. **Interview Preparation:**  
   You ask the chatbot to generate interview questions based on the JD.

6. **Offer Letter Drafting:**  
   Upon selection, you request the chatbot to draft an offer letter.

7. **Onboarding:**  
   You manually handle onboarding tasks.

### Limitations of Generative AI in Recruitment

- **Reactive:** The AI only acts on your commands; it cannot plan or initiate.
- **No Memory:** It does not retain past conversations or context.
- **Generic Outputs:** It produces standardised responses not tailored specifically to your company’s needs.
- **No Automation:** You perform all external actions manually.

---

## Enhancing Recruitment with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) Chatbots

To improve specificity and contextual relevance, companies can integrate their internal documents, past JDs, interview questions, salary bands, and hiring strategies into a **RAG-based chatbot**.

### Benefits of RAG Chatbots

- **Context-Aware Responses:** Tailored advice based on company-specific data.
- **Improved Shortlisting:** The chatbot analyses resumes against customised criteria.
- **Email Templates:** Generates emails consistent with company tone and style.

While this improves quality, the chatbot remains **reactive** and requires manual posting and scheduling.

---

## Fully Autonomous Recruitment with Agentic AI

The future of AI in recruitment is **agentic AI chatbots** that are:

- **Proactive:** They plan the entire hiring process from JD drafting to onboarding.
- **Contextual:** They remember previous steps and adapt accordingly.
- **Adaptive:** They monitor job posting performance and adjust strategies if applications are low.
- **Integrated:** They connect with APIs of LinkedIn, job portals, calendar systems, email servers, and HR management software to automate posting, scheduling, communication, and onboarding.

### How Agentic AI Executes Hiring

1. **Goal Recognition:** The chatbot understands the goal — hire a backend engineer with two to four years of experience.

2. **Planning:** It drafts a JD, posts it on relevant platforms via API, and monitors application inflow.

3. **Adaptation:** If applications are low, it revises the JD or promotes the posting.

4. **Shortlisting:** Automatically parses resumes, matches skills, and shortlists candidates.

5. **Scheduling Interviews:** Checks recruiter and candidate calendars, sends invites.

6. **Interview Assistance:** Provides interview questions based on company data.

7. **Offer Letter and Onboarding:** Drafts offer letters, sends emails, and triggers onboarding workflows.

### Advantages of Agentic AI

- **Reduces Human Workload:** Automates repetitive and administrative tasks.
- **Improves Efficiency:** Faster hiring cycles with less manual intervention.
- **Ensures Consistency:** Tailored to company policies and hiring practices.
- **Adapts in Real-Time:** Reacts to changes and suggests improvements proactively.

# The Evolution: From Generative AI to Agentic AI

The progression from generative AI to agentic AI represents a maturation of AI’s role from a **content creator** to an **autonomous agent** capable of managing and executing complex tasks independently. Agentic AI builds upon generative AI’s capabilities by adding reasoning, planning, and tool-use, making it indispensable for automating end-to-end workflows like recruitment.

---

# Conclusion: Embracing Agentic AI in Recruitment

Generative AI has undoubtedly revolutionised content creation and offered valuable tools for HR professionals. However, its reactive nature limits its role in automating entire recruitment cycles. Agentic AI bridges this gap by offering autonomous, context-aware, and adaptive solutions that reduce manual effort, increase efficiency, and improve hiring outcomes.

By integrating agentic AI chatbots with company data and external APIs, organisations can unlock a new level of intelligent automation. This not only streamlines recruitment but also empowers HR teams to focus on strategic decision-making rather than mundane tasks.

# FAQ: Generative AI vs Agentic AI in HR

**Q1: Can generative AI replace human recruiters?**  
Generative AI assists recruiters by generating content and answering queries but lacks autonomy and decision-making needed to replace human recruiters fully.

**Q2: What makes agentic AI ‘proactive’?**  
Agentic AI anticipates next steps, plans workflows, and initiates actions without waiting for user prompts.

**Q3: How does agentic AI maintain context?**  
It uses memory modules to retain past interactions and company-specific knowledge bases, enabling tailored responses and continuous task execution.

**Q4: Are agentic AI systems expensive to implement?**  
Costs vary, but leveraging existing APIs and company data can streamline implementation. The efficiency gains often justify the investment.
